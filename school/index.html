ABOUT

Our schools CTE program is the synthesis of standard STEM academic classes, our specialized Digital Media program, and a multitude of STEM related clubs for students to choose from. By utilizing these opportunities students can get a serious head-start in their career pathways, as well as have the potential to have great experience and achievements to show for it.

CURRICULUM

CURRICULUM

Digital Media: The Digital Media program is a CTE pathway that reconciles both the needs of a technical world and the growing emphasis on design seen in most careers in the present.

DM1- The introduction to the program consists of a crash course in basic design principles and learning the way around applications such as Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop. Projects include logo design and creating custom fonts.

DM1- A second-year course brings a new host of skills to students, giving them a chance to try their hand at journal and web design. Projects include a collaboratory, class-wide magazine as well as personal websites utilizing CSS and html.

DM1- Further expansions of the skill and experience help students move closer to the working, with in-the-field filmmaking being a core element of this year. Projects range from a student-directed and written film to 3D modeling and printing.

DM1- In their fourth and final year, students are free to pursue their own project, which is expected to be an experience which they use to gain real world connections and show the furthest extent of their skills.

IT and Repair- In addition to the comprehensive Digital Media program, students can opt into a year-long course where they assist with technology around the school, allowing them to earn technical certification.

STEM INTEGRATION

STEM INTEGRATION

To ensure students are able to investigate many different career pathways, our school offers a wide variety of intensive STEM courses. To do this we partner with another high school as well as several universities, extending our subject coverage to advanced biology, physics, mathematics, computer science, and engineering. Even more niche courses such as nanotechnology, astronomy, and cybersecurity are available to students who want to take a cheaper dive into their interests.

CLUBS/ACTIVITIES

CLUBS/ACTIVITIES

What really reinforces interest and involvement in CTE throughout our school is activities outside of school hours, which include clubs, trips, and competitions. STEM related clubs include TSA, Science Olympiad, Math League, and Odyssey of the Mind, and many students participate in physics, math, and engineering competitions. Additionally, trips such as the sophomore bio-trip give students the chance to get up close and personal with science and engineering.
